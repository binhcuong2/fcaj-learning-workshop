### General feedback

**1. Working environment**  
The environment of **Workforce Bootcamp - First Cloud AI Journey** was friendly, open, and supportive. During the program, I had the opportunity to learn, discuss, and practice through a real team project, the **AI Job Matching Platform**, built with AWS Serverless. This environment helped me become more confident when working with AWS services, technical documentation, and implementation issues.

**2. Mentor and team admin support**  
The mentors and team admins helped me better understand AWS services, workshop organization, and a more systematic way to approach technical problems. When I faced issues related to authentication, API Gateway, JWT Authorizer, frontend integration, or Hugo/GitHub Pages deployment, I had clearer guidance on how to check the flow step by step instead of only fixing errors by trial and error. This helped improve my debugging ability and problem-analysis mindset.

**3. Relevance to my academic major and career orientation**  
The workshop content matched my backend, cloud, and serverless orientation. Although some parts were challenging, especially when combining Cognito, API Gateway, Lambda, DynamoDB, S3, and frontend integration, these tasks helped me understand how a practical cloud-based system is designed and operated. This experience was valuable for both my academic learning and future career direction.

**4. Learning opportunities and skill development**  
Through the project, I improved my knowledge of AWS, serverless architecture, authentication, API Gateway, frontend authentication integration, Git/GitHub, and technical documentation. I also contributed to the **CV matching flow**, which helped me understand how frontend actions, APIs, Lambda functions, S3, DynamoDB, and AI-based CV evaluation steps work together. Besides technical skills, I also practiced problem solving, worklog writing, workshop documentation, and Hugo-based report deployment.

**5. Culture and teamwork**  
The teamwork culture in the program was positive. Team members could discuss, support one another, and share experience when facing issues or reviewing content. Working together on authentication, APIs, the CV matching flow, documentation, and deployment helped me better understand the importance of collaboration in a technical project.

**6. Internship policy and benefits**  
The program gave interns opportunities to learn through workshops, documentation, guidance, and hands-on practice. What I appreciated was that interns could work with a practical problem instead of only studying theory. I would like to have more opportunities to visit the office and experience the working environment directly.

### Additional questions

**What I was most satisfied with**  
What I was most satisfied with was the opportunity to participate in a practical project that used multiple AWS services and had a clear business workflow. Parts such as Cognito Authentication, API Gateway with JWT Authorizer, frontend authentication integration, and the CV matching flow helped me better understand how components in a serverless system connect with each other.

**What could be improved for future interns**  
I would like to have more opportunities to visit the office and experience the working environment directly. This would allow interns to interact more with mentors, admins, and other participants, better understand the workplace culture, build stronger connections, and learn more from the real working environment.

**Whether I would recommend the program to friends**  
I would recommend the program to friends who are interested in cloud, backend development, or AWS Serverless. The program is suitable for those who want to learn through hands-on practice, teamwork, and building a practical project. However, participants should be ready to self-study, read documentation, and proactively ask questions when they encounter problems.

### Suggestions and expectations

- I would like to have more opportunities to visit the office and experience the working environment directly.
- This would allow interns to interact more with mentors, admins, and other participants, better understand the workplace culture, build stronger connections, and learn more from the real working environment.
- I hope the program continues to maintain an open, practical learning environment that encourages interns to solve problems proactively.
